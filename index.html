Bio

I am a Ph.D student with the state key laboratory of industrial control technology at Zhejiang University, under the supervision of Prof. Zhi Wang. I received the B.E. degree in automation from Tianjin University in 2015. From Nov. 2018 to Nov. 2019, I was a visiting Ph.D student with the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science, the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, where I conducted research under the supervision of Prof. Hairong Qi (IEEE Fellow).

Research

My research interests lie in incentive mechanism design, task allocation, privacy protection, and data aggregation in networked sensing systems. The research goal is to achieve efficient, reliable, secure, and privacy-preserving data collection in Internet of Things (IoT) and cyber physical systems (CPS). Previously, I conducted research on compressive sensing and its applications in sensor networks and array signal processing. Currently, I am particularly interested in the field of distributed machine learning, especially federated learning.
